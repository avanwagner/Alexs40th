I · Preface

A Note to the Gentleman Reader

What follows is an itinerary, presented in three acts, for the marking of a fortieth year. It has been compiled with the discretion appropriate to the occasion and the indiscretion appropriate to the company.

You are asked to arrive on time, to dress as instructed, and to conduct yourselves — broadly — as gentlemen of stature … and a bureaucrat. The remainder is left to your good judgment. Such as it is.

IV · The Code

House Rules

  1. What happens in the suite, the cart, the spa, and the steakhouse remains in the suite, the cart, the spa, and the steakhouse.

  2. Suits Thursday night. No exceptions. Cufflinks encouraged.

  3. The standing of moron is honored at all times. Dignity is observed only when wearing a tie.

  4. Phones face down at table. Photographs permitted before the second drink.

  5. Friday's evening programme is the sole jurisdiction of Jamby and Dom. Derek is to be politely, firmly, and without exception kept away from it.

  6. The gentleman returning Saturday's highest score shall submit to one indignity, the nature of which is to be determined by the better-playing morons.

  7. To the gentleman among us with the catalogue of travel grievances — pillow firmness, water temperature, the proximity of windows, the timing of meals — this weekend, the catalogue stays closed. We have heard it. We love you. Not another word.
